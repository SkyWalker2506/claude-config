# Google Reviews System Guidelines

## What the Reviews System Evaluates

Google's Reviews System rewards high-quality review content that provides:
- Insightful analysis and original research
- Expert or enthusiast perspective with genuine knowledge

The system evaluates:
- Articles, blog posts, pages with the purpose of providing a recommendation, opinion, or analysis
- Single-item reviews, head-to-head comparisons, and ranked-list recommendations
- Any topic: products, media, services, businesses

It does NOT evaluate third-party user reviews in product review sections.

## High-Quality Review Criteria

### Expert Demonstration
- Show you are knowledgeable — don't just summarize specs
- Provide evidence: visuals, audio, or other links showing your own experience
- Share quantitative measurements across performance categories

### Differentiation and Depth
- Explain what sets something apart from competitors
- Cover comparable alternatives and when each is best
- Discuss benefits AND drawbacks based on original research
- Describe design choices and their effect on users (beyond manufacturer claims)

### Decision-Making Focus
- Focus on the most important decision-making factors from experience
- Example: a car review identifies fuel economy and safety as key factors
- Describe how a product has evolved from previous versions

### Content Structure Best Practices
- Include links to other useful resources (own or external)
- For ranked lists: ensure enough standalone useful content per item
- When recommending "best overall": include why, with first-hand supporting evidence

## Review Quality Assessment Framework

When analyzing review content, evaluate:

1. **Author Expertise** — Is the author authoritative for this topic?
2. **Experience Evidence** — Does content demonstrate first-hand experience?
3. **Depth vs. Thin Content** — Original analysis vs. product spec summaries?
4. **User Perspective** — Is it evaluated from a user's benefit lens?
5. **Freshness** — Check "Last Updated" date; outdated reviews lose value
6. **Comparative Value** — Does it help readers choose between options?

## EEAT in Reviews Context

- EEAT (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness) is NOT a direct ranking factor
- But it informs quality rater assessments which influence algorithm updates
- Reviews should demonstrate first-hand experience (the "E" in EEAT)

## Areas of Improvement Checklist

For any review content audit:
- [ ] Does the author demonstrate genuine expertise?
- [ ] Is there original research beyond what manufacturers publish?
- [ ] Are quantitative comparisons included?
- [ ] Are benefits AND drawbacks covered honestly?
- [ ] Are links to external resources included?
- [ ] Is the "last updated" date visible and recent?
- [ ] For ranked lists: does each item have standalone useful content?
- [ ] For "best overall" picks: is the reasoning evidence-based?
