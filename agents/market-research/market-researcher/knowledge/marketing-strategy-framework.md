# Marketing Strategy Framework

## Marketing Plan Structure

A comprehensive marketing plan follows this sequence:

### 1. Business and Goals Assessment
- Define the business, products/services clearly
- Set SMART goals: Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound
- Example: "Increase trial signups by 30% in Q3 via organic search"

### 2. Target Audience Analysis
- **Demographics**: Age, location, role, company size
- **Psychographics**: Values, motivations, pain points, aspirations
- **Buying behaviors**: Decision triggers, research process, objections

### 3. Competitive Analysis
Assess each competitor across:
- Strengths (what they do well)
- Weaknesses (gaps, poor reviews, missing features)
- Market positioning (premium/budget, niche/broad)
- Distribution and go-to-market channels

### 4. SWOT Analysis
| | Internal | External |
|---|---|---|
| **Positive** | Strengths | Opportunities |
| **Negative** | Weaknesses | Threats |

### 5. Strategy Selection Matrix

| Strategy | Best For |
|----------|---------|
| Content marketing | Long-term SEO, thought leadership |
| Social media campaigns | Awareness, community building |
| Email marketing | Retention, nurture sequences |
| SEO/SEM | Intent-driven acquisition |
| Traditional advertising | Mass awareness, local markets |
| Partnerships | New audience reach |

### 6. Budget Allocation Framework
- Allocate by expected ROI per channel
- Reserve 10-20% for experimentation
- Track CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost) per channel
- Compare LTV:CAC ratio minimum 3:1

### 7. Implementation Timeline
- Organize in sprints (2-4 week cycles)
- Define key milestones and owners
- Set leading indicators (clicks, trials) and lagging indicators (revenue)

### 8. Monitoring and Evaluation
Key metrics to track:
- **Awareness**: Impressions, reach, brand search volume
- **Acquisition**: CAC, conversion rate, trial starts
- **Retention**: Churn rate, NPS, engagement rate
- **Revenue**: MRR, ARR, LTV

## Market Research Methods

### Primary Research
- Customer interviews (5-10 for qualitative insight)
- Surveys (statistical significance: n>100 for most segments)
- User testing and usability studies

### Secondary Research
- Industry reports (Gartner, Forrester, Statista)
- Competitor pricing pages, job postings (reveal priorities)
- App store reviews, G2/Capterra reviews
- Google Trends for demand signals

## Differentiation Framework

To stand out in competitive analysis, answer:
1. What does no one else do that we do?
2. What do we do better than everyone else?
3. Who do we serve that others ignore?
4. What would customers lose most if we disappeared?
