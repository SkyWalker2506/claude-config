# Startup Strategy Frameworks

## SWOT Analysis

Analyze using 3-4 bullet points per quadrant:

| Quadrant | Focus |
|----------|-------|
| **Strengths** | Internal advantages — what you do better than competitors |
| **Weaknesses** | Internal limitations — resource gaps, capability gaps |
| **Opportunities** | External factors you can exploit — market trends, gaps |
| **Threats** | External risks — competitive pressure, regulatory, macro |

**For tech startups specifically:**
- Strengths: technical moat, team expertise, IP, network effects
- Weaknesses: burn rate, missing distribution, narrow market
- Opportunities: emerging platforms, underserved segments, API ecosystems
- Threats: big tech entering space, commoditization, talent acquisition cost

## PEST Analysis

Macro-environment scan across 4 dimensions:

| Factor | Key Questions |
|--------|--------------|
| **Political** | Regulatory risk, government support/opposition, trade policy |
| **Economic** | Market size, funding environment, customer purchasing power |
| **Social** | Demographics, cultural shifts, behavior changes, adoption curves |
| **Technological** | Enabling tech, disruption risk, platform dependency |

## Porter's Five Forces

Competitive intensity analysis:

1. **Threat of New Entrants** — Barriers to entry, capital requirements, brand loyalty needed
2. **Bargaining Power of Suppliers** — Dependency on key vendors, switching costs
3. **Bargaining Power of Buyers** — Customer concentration, price sensitivity, substitutes
4. **Threat of Substitutes** — Alternative solutions, switching ease, price-performance tradeoffs
5. **Competitive Rivalry** — Number of competitors, growth rate, differentiation level

## Application Workflow

For any strategic analysis request:
1. Identify the company/product stage (pre-product, post-PMF, scaling)
2. Run PEST first (macro context sets the stage)
3. Apply Five Forces (competitive dynamics)
4. Synthesize in SWOT (internal + external combined view)
5. Derive 2-3 strategic priorities from the synthesis

## Key Principle

- Keep analysis concise and directly relevant to the tech startup sector
- Avoid broad or non-specific information — every point should be actionable
- Prioritize the most critical 3-4 factors per framework, not exhaustive lists
