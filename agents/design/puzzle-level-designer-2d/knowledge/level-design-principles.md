# Level Design Principles (Universal)

## 1. Goal clear, process interesting
Player should understand what they are trying to achieve before being asked to solve how.

## 2. One primary design intention
Every level must have a main purpose:
- teach
- practice
- test
- combine
- twist
- pace break (breather)
- mastery challenge
- narrative beat (only if gameplay remains readable)

## 3. Level = decisions, not assets
A level is a sequence of player decisions shaped by space, mechanics, risk/reward and feedback — not a pile of obstacles.

## 4. Every gameplay object needs a purpose
Each obstacle/platform/hazard/enemy/goal must serve gameplay, readability, pacing, theme, or atmosphere.

## 5. Failure must teach
A failed attempt should reveal information (constraint, rule, wrong assumption), not just punish.

## 6. Teach before test
Never require a mechanic, rule, enemy behavior or traversal skill before the player has learned it.

## 7. Constrain before combining
New ideas should appear in simplified context before combining with other mechanics.

## 8. Spatial logic is mandatory
Objects must be physically and visually plausible according to the game’s rules and surfaces.

## 9. Readability beats cleverness
A clever level that players cannot parse is a failed level.

## 10. Validate before final
No level proposal is complete until spatial, gameplay, readability, difficulty and game-pack validation pass.
