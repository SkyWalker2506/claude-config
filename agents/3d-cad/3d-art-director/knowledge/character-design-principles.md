# Character Design Principles

## 1. Shape Language — The Visual Alphabet

Every character starts with three primitive shapes. These shapes carry universal emotional meaning before a single detail is added.

### Core Shape Map

| Shape | Emotion | Body Language | Use For |
|-------|---------|---------------|---------|
| **Circle** | Friendly, safe, warm, approachable | Soft curves, rounded shoulders, plump forms | Heroes, sidekicks, children, comedic characters |
| **Square** | Stable, strong, reliable, stubborn | Broad shoulders, flat tops, blocky limbs | Warriors, protectors, mentors, tanks |
| **Triangle** | Dangerous, dynamic, aggressive, cunning | Pointed features, tapered limbs, sharp angles | Villains, rogues, predators, speedsters |

### Shape Combination Rules

Characters are never one pure shape. Blend shapes with a dominant (60-70%) and secondary (30-40%):

| Combination | Reads As | Example Archetype |
|-------------|----------|-------------------|
| Circle + Square | Gentle giant, trustworthy tank | Dwarf blacksmith, friendly golem |
| Circle + Triangle | Mischievous, trickster | Goblin, imp, jester |
| Square + Triangle | Imposing warrior, dangerous strength | Orc warlord, armored knight |
| Triangle + Circle | Cunning but charismatic | Dark elf assassin, sorcerer |
| Square + Circle | Immovable, motherly/fatherly | Elder dwarf, earth elemental |

### 3D-Specific Shape Application

- **Think in volumes, not outlines**: Circles become spheres/ellipsoids, squares become cubes/cylinders, triangles become cones/pyramids
- **Shape reads from ALL angles**: A character must be recognizable from front, side, back, and 3/4 view
- **Shape language extends to movement**: Round characters bounce and roll; angular characters slash and dart; square characters plant and hold

## 2. Exaggeration — What to Push, What to Ground

### The 70/30 Rule

Push 70% of features toward exaggeration, keep 30% grounded in reality. The grounded elements anchor believability; the exaggerated elements create personality.

### What to Exaggerate (Push)

| Feature | Direction | Effect |
|---------|-----------|--------|
| Head size | Larger (+20-50%) | More expressive, younger, approachable |
| Hands | Larger (+15-30%) | More gestural, capable, powerful |
| Jaw/chin | Heavier or sharper | Strength or cunning |
| Brow ridge | Deeper, more prominent | Intensity, aggression, wisdom |
| Shoulders | Wider (1.5-3x head width) | Power, heroism |
| Waist | Narrower than shoulders | Dynamic V-shape for action characters |
| Limb length | Shorter or longer than real | Short = sturdy/comedic; Long = elegant/creepy |

### What to Keep Grounded (Anchor)

- **Joint placement**: Elbows at waist, wrists at crotch, knees at mid-leg — these stay anatomically correct even in stylized designs
- **Weight distribution**: Characters must look like they can stand. Center of gravity over feet.
- **Eye placement**: Eyes at the vertical midpoint of the head (or slightly lower for cute characters)
- **Finger count and bend direction**: Even in exaggerated designs, hands need to look functional

### Exaggeration Scale by Style

| Style | Exaggeration Level | Head-Body Distortion |
|-------|-------------------|---------------------|
| Realistic | 0-10% | Subtle proportional tweaks |
| Semi-realistic | 10-25% | Slightly larger head, broader shoulders |
| Stylized | 25-50% | Noticeable push on key features |
| Cartoon | 50-100% | Major distortion for personality |
| Chibi/SD | 100-200% | Head = 1/2 to 1/3 of total height |

## 3. Readability — The 64px Thumbnail Test

A well-designed character must be instantly recognizable when reduced to a 64x64 pixel thumbnail (roughly the size of a game minimap icon or distant LOD).

### Readability Checklist

1. **Fill the character with solid black** — can you tell who it is from the silhouette alone?
2. **Reduce to 64px** — do the major shapes still read clearly?
3. **Compare against other characters** — is each one distinct at thumbnail size?
4. **Check all 4 rotations** — front, side, back, 3/4 all need to read

### Design Decisions That Improve Readability

| Decision | Impact |
|----------|--------|
| One dominant shape per character | Instant category recognition |
| Asymmetric elements (one big shoulder pad, off-center horn) | Breaks symmetry = more memorable |
| High contrast value areas (dark body + light face) | Eye drawn to important features |
| Distinctive headgear/hair/horns | Silhouette differentiation at distance |
| Large simple accessories over small complex ones | Reads at any scale |
| Clear negative space between limbs and body | Prevents "blob" silhouette |

### What Destroys Readability

- Too many small details at the same scale (visual noise)
- Symmetrical designs with no unique features
- Monochrome value (everything the same brightness)
- Limbs merged with body (no negative space)
- Over-detailed surface texture that flattens at distance

## 4. Personality Through Form

### How Body Shape Tells Story

| Body Shape | Personality Signal | Fantasy Application |
|------------|-------------------|---------------------|
| Pear (wide hips, narrow shoulders) | Grounded, maternal, sturdy | Dwarf matriarch, earth spirit |
| Inverted triangle (wide shoulders, narrow hips) | Powerful, heroic, dominant | Orc chieftain, paladin |
| Rectangle (equal width throughout) | Dependable, simple, honest | Human soldier, golem |
| Hourglass (wide shoulders + hips, narrow waist) | Dynamic, balanced, agile | Elf ranger, demon hunter |
| Oval (rounded, wider in middle) | Jovial, wealthy, lazy or wise | Merchant, tavern keeper, sage |
| Stick (thin throughout) | Intellectual, frail, fast | Lich, scholar, wraith |

### Posture Communicates Status

- **Straight spine, chin up**: Confident, noble, arrogant
- **Hunched forward**: Cunning, sneaky, burdened
- **Leaning back**: Casual, lazy, dismissive
- **Asymmetric lean**: Injured, relaxed, cocky

## 5. Fantasy Archetype Design Traits

### Goblin

- **Head count**: 3.5-4.5 heads tall (head = ~25% of body height)
- **Shape language**: Triangle (dominant) + Circle (secondary) — cunning + mischievous
- **Key features**: Oversized pointed ears (bat-like), large eyes relative to skull, long nose, small pointed teeth, receding chin
- **Body**: Long arms relative to torso, short bowed legs, narrow shoulders, hunched forward posture, thin limbs with knobby joints
- **Skull reference**: Tarsier-like — flat triangular skull with massive eye sockets
- **Color**: Yellow-green to grey-green skin, yellow/red eyes
- **Personality through form**: Forward lean = scheming; long fingers = grasping/thieving

### Orc

- **Head count**: 6-7 heads tall (stockier than human)
- **Shape language**: Square (dominant) + Triangle (secondary) — strong + dangerous
- **Key features**: Heavy brow ridge, underbite with protruding tusks, broad flat nose, small deep-set eyes, thick neck
- **Body**: Massive shoulders (2.5-3x head width), barrel chest, thick forearms, short legs relative to torso
- **Skull reference**: Gorilla-like jaw structure, heavy supraorbital ridge
- **Color**: Brown-green to dark olive skin, darker on exposed surfaces
- **Personality through form**: Wide stance = territorial; forward shoulders = aggressive readiness

### Elf

- **Head count**: 8-9 heads tall (elongated and graceful)
- **Shape language**: Triangle (dominant, inverted/elegant) + Circle (secondary) — dynamic + ethereal
- **Key features**: Pointed ears (leaf-shaped), angular face, high cheekbones, almond eyes, small nose and mouth, long neck
- **Body**: Lean and elongated, narrow shoulders, long limbs, minimal body fat, flowing lines
- **Skull reference**: Refined human skull with elongated cranium, prominent cheekbones
- **Color**: Pale to amber skin, blemishless; hair ranges white to black
- **Personality through form**: Upright posture = noble; elongated limbs = otherworldly grace

### Dwarf

- **Head count**: 4.5-5.5 heads tall
- **Shape language**: Square (dominant) + Circle (secondary) — stable + friendly
- **Key features**: Heavy brow, broad flat nose bridge with bulbous tip, wide heavy jaw, thick beard (males), small deep-set eyes
- **Body**: Extremely broad shoulders (3x head width), barrel torso, thick short limbs, massive hands, dense bone structure
- **Skull reference**: Flatter, longer toward back; heavy sloping brows like archaic hominids
- **Color**: Ruddy to pale skin; red, brown, or black hair/beard
- **Personality through form**: Low center of gravity = immovable; thick limbs = built for labor

### Dragon (Humanoid/Drake)

- **Head count**: Variable (6-10+ depending on style)
- **Shape language**: Triangle (dominant) + Square (secondary) — dangerous + powerful
- **Key features**: Horns, elongated snout, vertical slit pupils, armored scales, dorsal spines, tail, wing membranes
- **Body**: Long neck, powerful chest, digitigrade legs, clawed hands, tail as counterbalance
- **Color**: Typically matches elemental affinity (red=fire, blue=ice, green=poison, black=shadow)
- **Personality through form**: Upright = regal/ancient; crouched = feral/predatory
